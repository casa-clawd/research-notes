Research Findings

Source 1: Renogy AU - 200Ah LiFePO4 Battery

URL: https://au.renogy.com/products/deep-cycle-batteries/lithium-batteries/

Credibility: High — Major brand, AU store, local warranty

Key points:

  • 12.8V 200Ah LiFePO4: AUD$729-899 (on sale)
  • Low-temperature protection model available (critical for Killarney)
  • 5-year warranty
  • Free shipping Australia-wide

Notes: Price is competitive with imports when you factor in warranty and local support. Low-temp model is essential for Killarney winters.

Source 2: LiTime AU - 200Ah LiFePO4

URL: https://au.litime.com/products/litime-12v-200ah-plus-lifepo4-lithium-battery

Credibility: High — Popular budget brand, AU store

Key points:

  • 12V 200Ah Plus with 200A BMS
  • Usually cheaper than Renogy
  • Bluetooth monitoring on some models
  • Fast AU shipping

Notes: Check if model has low-temp cutoff. Good reviews in caravan community. Worth comparing prices with Renogy.

Source 3: Gumtree Brisbane - Used Solar Panels

URL: https://www.gumtree.com.au/s-brisbane/used+solar+panels/k0l3005721

Credibility: Medium — Marketplace, buyer beware

Key points:

  • Regular listings: 250-400W panels at $50-150 each
  • Example: 22x Canadian Solar 300W panels (5 years old) for ~$50-80/panel
  • Often from system upgrades (panels still good)
  • Pickup only usually (Brisbane/Gold Coast area)

Notes: Check listings weekly. Good deals go fast. Bring multimeter to test Voc before handing over cash.

Source 4: Domino Clamps - Australia Shipping

URL: https://dominoclamps.com/products/domino-clamp

Credibility: High — Purpose-built product, real AU customers

Key points:

  • 4-6 working days shipping to Australia
  • Shipping to AU/NZ is HALF PRICE currently
  • Multiple bracket options for solar mounting
  • No drilling required—clamps to corner castings

Notes: ~AUD$200-300 for 4x clamps + brackets. Worth it for no-drill peace of mind. Can reposition if needed.

Source 5: Renogy AU - Rover MPPT 30A

URL: https://au.renogy.com/rover-li-30-amp-mppt-solar-charge-controller/

Credibility: High — AU store, local support

Key points:

  • Rover Li 30A MPPT: AUD$134-180
  • 40A version also available (~AUD$200)
  • Bluetooth monitoring built-in
  • Handles 12V/24V systems

Notes: 30A sufficient for 400W array. Bluetooth useful for checking performance without going to container.

Source 6: Victron SmartSolar MPPT 100/30

URL: https://solaronline.com.au/victron-smartsolar-100v-30a-mppt-regulator.html

Credibility: High — Industry standard, AU supplier

Key points:

  • Superior build quality vs Renogy
  • Bluetooth Smart built-in
  • Better MPPT algorithm (especially in partial shade)

Notes: If budget allows, Victron is the “buy once, cry once” choice. Better for harsh conditions.

Climate Considerations for Killarney

Sun Hours by Season

  • Summer: ~6-7 peak hours
  • Winter: ~3-4 peak hours
  • Design for winter: Need larger array or accept generator backup

Frost Risk

  • Killarney gets 30-50 frost days/year
  • LiFePO4 batteries cannot charge below 0°C
  • Solutions:
    1. Buy battery with built-in low-temp protection (Renogy Mini)
    2. Insulate battery box
    3. Small 12V heater pad (thermostat controlled)
    4. Accept no charging on coldest nights (battery still discharges fine)

Hail Risk

  • SE QLD gets severe storms
  • Secondhand panels may not have warranty
  • Consider insurance or accept risk

Patterns & Themes

  1. Australian market is mature — Plenty of local suppliers, don’t need to import
  2. Secondhand panels available — Brisbane/Gold Coast active market
  3. LiFePO4 prices dropping — Now competitive with lead-acid over lifespan
  4. Frost is the gotcha — Easy to overlook, critical for battery longevity
  5. Weekend project viable — Modular components, can install in stages
