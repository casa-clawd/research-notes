Executive Summary

Bottom Line

A 12V off-grid solar system on your Killarney container is very doable and well-suited to the climate. Using secondhand panels from Gumtree/Facebook Marketplace can cut costs significantly. For lights + fridge, budget AUD$1,800-2,400 for a reliable LiFePO4 system, or AUD$1,400-1,800 with lead-acid (shorter lifespan, more maintenance).

Key Findings

  1. Killarney sun hours are good: 4.5-5.5 peak sun hours, but winter performance drops ~30%
  2. Frost risk matters: LiFePO4 batteries can’t charge below 0°C—need heating or insulation in winter
  3. Panel mounting is solved: Domino Clamps ship to Australia (4-6 days), no drilling required
  4. 12V fridges are efficient: Modern compressor fridges use 40-80W, ~1-1.5kWh/day—very manageable
  5. Secondhand panels viable in QLD: Gumtree/FB Marketplace have regular listings at $50-100 per 250-300W panel
  6. MPPT worth it: 5-30% more power, especially important in suboptimal winter conditions

Recommendation

Go with LiFePO4 + MPPT despite higher upfront cost. Killarney’s frost risk means you need low-temperature protection (many LiFePO4 models have this built-in). Source panels secondhand from Brisbane/Gold Coast listings. Use Domino Clamps for no-drill mounting.

Confidence Level

  • High — Multiple sources agree, local market confirmed, climate data available
