Governance & Safeguards

Australian Government AI Governance Framework

Current Policy Landscape (2025)

1. Australia’s AI Ethics Principles (2019)

  • Human, social and environmental wellbeing
  • Human-centered values
  • Fairness
  • Privacy protection and security
  • Reliability and safety
  • Transparency and explainability
  • Contestability
  • Accountability

2. Policy for Responsible AI in Government (v2.0, December 2025)

  • Mandatory for Australian Government agencies
  • Risk-based approach to AI use
  • Transparency requirements
  • Human oversight mandates

3. Services Australia AI Strategy 2025-27

  • Governance and assurance processes before deployment
  • Transparency statement on AI use
  • Alignment with whole-of-government frameworks

Safeguards Against Algorithmic Agentic States

Technical Safeguards

SafeguardPurpose
Explainable AI (XAI)Officials can understand AI reasoning
Confidence ScoringFlag uncertain predictions
Audit LogsTrack all AI decisions and overrides
Bias TestingIdentify discriminatory patterns

Human Safeguards

SafeguardPurpose
Mandatory Override TrainingStaff know they can question AI
Human-in-the-LoopRequired human review for high-stakes
Whistleblower ProtectionsSafe to raise concerns

Red Flags: When Governance is Failing

Technical Red Flags

  • AI decisions can’t be explained to affected citizens
  • No confidence scores or uncertainty measures
  • No audit trail of decisions

Cultural Red Flags

  • “The algorithm decided” is common phrase
  • Staff stop questioning AI outputs
  • “Just following the system” defenses

Recommendations for Australian Government

Immediate Actions

  1. Expand Robodebt Royal Commission recommendations

    • Apply lessons to all government AI
    • Mandatory human review for welfare decisions

  2. Implement Canada-style risk classification

    • Level 1-4 based on impact on citizens
    • Stricter requirements for higher-risk AI

  3. Establish Independent AI Auditor

    • Non-departmental body
    • Annual audits of all government AI

Medium-Term Reforms

  1. Create Citizen Appeal Pathways

    • Right to human review of AI decisions
    • Accessible, timely, free

  2. Train Public Servants on Automation Bias

    • Mandatory training on algorithmic agentic states
    • Real case studies (Robodebt, etc.)

The Bottom Line

Algorithmic agentic states are preventable with:

  • Technical transparency (explainable AI)
  • Human empowerment (override authority)
  • Organizational accountability (independent audits)
  • Citizen rights (appeal processes)

Australia has the policy framework. Implementation will determine whether Robodebt was a lesson learned or a warning ignored.
