Two Meanings of Agentic State

Meaning 1: The Psychological Agentic State (Milgram)

Origin: Stanley Milgram’s obedience experiments (1963)

Definition: A mental state where an individual acts as an agent of an authority figure, deferring moral responsibility upward. The person sees themselves as an instrument carrying out another’s wishes, not as a morally responsible individual.

In government AI context: Public servants deferring to algorithmic recommendations, rubber-stamping AI decisions, shifting responsibility to “the system.”

Meaning 2: The Governance Agentic State (Ilves et al., 2025)

Origin: Vision paper by Global Government Technology Centre Berlin & The World Bank (October 2025)

Definition: “A comprehensive transformation of public administration where autonomous systems handle complexity and scale while human officials focus on strategic direction, political accountability, and decisions requiring judgment.”

Core thesis: Governments must transform into “Agentic States” to remain effective and legitimate in the AI era.

The Paradox: When the Agentic State Creates Agentic States

The Risk

The transformation to an Agentic State (governance model) may inadvertently create agentic states (psychological phenomenon) in public servants.

How the Paradox Manifests

Scenario 1: The Rubber-Stamp Official

  • AI flags a benefit applicant as high-risk
  • Caseworker approves without review because “the system calculated it”
  • False negative harms vulnerable citizen
  • Official: “I was following the system”

Scenario 2: The Accountability Gap

  • AI-driven decision causes harm
  • Department: “The algorithm made the decision”
  • Vendor: “We just built the system”
  • Minister: “I wasn’t aware of the technical details”
  • No one is accountable

Ilves et al.’s Framework for Managing the Paradox

The 6-Level Autonomy Spectrum

LevelDescriptionHuman RoleRisk of Agentic State
0Manual (human-only)Full controlNone
1Rule-based automationSupervisionLow
2Intelligent process automationOversightLow-Medium
3Agentic workflowsCollaborationMedium
4Semi-autonomous agentsException handlingHigh
5Fully autonomous agentsStrategic direction onlyVery High

Current state: Most production systems at Level 3 (agentic workflows) Risk zone: Levels 4-5 where human oversight diminishes

Key Safeguards Proposed

  1. Outcome-based measurement: Define what success looks like before deploying AI
  2. Accountability frameworks: Clear chains of responsibility for AI decisions
  3. Safety and redress mechanisms: Citizens can challenge and correct AI errors
  4. People, culture, and leadership: Train officials to collaborate with AI, not defer to it
