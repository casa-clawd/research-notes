Case Studies

Case Study 1: Robodebt (Australia, 2016-2020)

What Happened

The Australian Government implemented an automated debt recovery system for Centrelink welfare payments:

  • Algorithm compared fortnightly welfare data with annual tax data
  • Income averaging created false debts for people with irregular income
  • 470,000+ incorrect debt notices issued
  • A$1.8 billion unlawfully claimed
  • Multiple suicides linked to the scheme

The Algorithmic Agentic State in Action

The Authority: The “system”—automated debt calculation algorithm

The Agentic Shift:

  • Centrelink staff: “The system calculated the debt”
  • Department officials: “We’re following the process”
  • Ministers: “The data matching identified discrepancies”

Moral Strain Ignored:

  • Public servants raised concerns internally from 2016
  • Staff knew averaging logic was flawed
  • Continued processing debts despite knowing errors

Key Lessons

  1. Automation bias was rampant — Staff trusted algorithm over common sense
  2. Human oversight failed — Rubber-stamping, not real review
  3. Scale of harm massive — 470,000+ people affected simultaneously
  4. Responsibility evaporated — No one held accountable until Royal Commission

Case Study 2: Dutch Tax Authority Childcare Benefits Scandal

Dutch tax authority used algorithmic risk profiling to detect childcare benefit fraud:

  • System flagged parents with certain nationalities as high-risk
  • 26,000+ parents falsely accused of fraud
  • Families forced to repay tens of thousands of euros
  • Many families entered financial ruin, some committed suicide

Key Parallel to Robodebt

Human officials amplified algorithmic bias rather than preventing it—automation bias in action.

Case Study 3: UK A-Level Algorithm (2020)

During COVID-19, UK used algorithm to assign A-level grades when exams were cancelled:

  • Algorithm downgraded 40% of teacher-predicted grades
  • Downgrades correlated with school type (state vs. private)
  • Massive public outcry
  • Government reversed course within days

What Was Different

Public outcry was immediate and intense. The agentic state broke down quickly when affected population was middle-class with political power.

Comparative Analysis

CaseDomainHarm ScaleDurationOutcome
RobodebtWelfare470,000+ people4 yearsRoyal Commission
Dutch TaxBenefits26,000 families5+ yearsGovernment resigned
UK A-LevelsEducation~300,000 studentsDaysImmediate reversal

Pattern Recognition

What enables algorithmic agentic states:

  1. Affected population is marginalized
  2. High technical complexity (“trust the algorithm”)
  3. Efficiency pressures
  4. Lack of transparency
  5. No clear accountability chain
