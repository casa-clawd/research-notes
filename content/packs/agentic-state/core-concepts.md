## The Original Agentic State (Milgram)

Stanley Milgram's obedience experiments revealed that ordinary people will harm others when:
1. An authority figure gives orders
2. The person enters an **agentic state** (acts as instrument of authority)
3. Responsibility is deferred upward
4. **Moral strain** is experienced but overridden

Key insight: It's not cruelty that enables harm—it's surrendering moral agency.

## The Algorithmic Agentic State

When AI enters government decision-making, a parallel phenomenon emerges:

| Element | Milgram's Lab | Government AI |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Authority** | Experimenter in lab coat | Algorithm/system output |
| **Command** | "Continue the shocks" | "System flagged this case" |
| **Agentic shift** | "I'm following orders" | "The algorithm decided" |
| **Responsibility** | Deferred to experimenter | Deferred to system/vendor |
| **Moral strain** | Visible distress | Known errors, continued use |
| **Defense** | "I was just following orders" | "I was following the system" |

## Automation Bias

**Definition**: The tendency to over-rely on automated recommendations and ignore contradictory information, even when correct.

**Why it happens**:
- Cognitive miserliness (easier to accept than question)
- Perceived superiority of AI analytical ability
- Time pressure in bureaucratic environments
- Lack of transparency in AI reasoning

## Algorithmic Authority

The perceived legitimacy of AI systems derives from:

1. **Technical mystique**: "The algorithm is complex and data-driven"
2. **Institutional backing**: Government-endorsed system
3. **Consistency**: AI applies rules uniformly (apparently)
4. **Efficiency**: Faster than human processing
5. **Objectivity myth**: "Algorithms are unbiased" (they're not)

## The Responsibility Gap

When AI makes decisions, accountability becomes diffuse:

- **Vendor**: "We just built the system"
- **Department**: "We followed the vendor's guidance"
- **Official**: "The algorithm flagged this"
- **Minister**: "I wasn't aware of the technical details"
- **Citizen**: Harmed with no clear recourse

## Moral Strain in the Digital Bureaucracy

Signs that officials are experiencing moral strain with AI systems:
- Knowing the AI is producing false positives but continuing to use it
- Expressing concerns in meetings but not in writing
- "Working around" the system rather than fixing it
- High staff turnover in AI-dependent teams
- Whistleblower reports (often after harm occurs)

**Robodebt example**: Multiple public servants raised concerns internally; system continued for years.
