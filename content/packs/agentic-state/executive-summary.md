## Bottom Line

Stanley Milgram's **agentic state**—where people surrender moral responsibility to authority figures—has a direct parallel in modern government AI systems. When public servants defer to algorithmic recommendations, they enter an **algorithmic agentic state**: responsibility shifts to "the system," critical thinking atrophies, and harmful decisions get rubber-stamped.

**New insight from Ilves et al. (2025)**: There's a second meaning of "Agentic State" emerging—the transformation of government itself through autonomous AI systems. This creates a paradox: the Agentic State (as governance model) may inadvertently create agentic states (as psychological phenomenon) in public servants.

## The Core Parallel

**Milgram's Laboratory (1963)** → **Government AI (2020s)**
- "The experimenter told me to" → "The algorithm flagged this"
- 65% obeyed to lethal voltage → Officials approved thousands of false Robodebts
- Moral strain visible but ignored → Staff knew system was flawed but continued

## The New Vision: Ilves et al.'s Agentic State

A comprehensive transformation where:
- Autonomous systems handle complexity and scale
- Human officials focus on strategic direction and political accountability
- Shift from "doing things right" (procedural compliance) to "doing the right things" (outcome optimization)
- "The most significant shift in how governments operate since the rise of modern bureaucracy"

## The Paradox

The Agentic State (governance transformation) may create agentic states (psychological surrender) if:
- Officials defer to AI recommendations without critical evaluation
- "The system decided" becomes an accountability shield
- Automation bias leads to rubber-stamping of AI outputs
- Human judgment atrophies as AI handles more decisions

## Key Risks

1. **Automation Bias**: Officials favor AI recommendations over their own judgment
2. **Responsibility Diffusion**: "The algorithm decided" becomes accountability shield
3. **Deskilling**: Critical thinking atrophies as AI handles decisions
4. **Scale of Harm**: Algorithmic errors affect thousands simultaneously (Robodebt: 470,000+ people)
5. **The Paradox**: Agentic State governance may create agentic state psychology
