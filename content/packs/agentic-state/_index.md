> How Milgram's concept of the agentic state illuminates the risks of algorithmic authority in government decision-making, from Robodebt to the era of Agentic AI. Now incorporating insights from Ilves et al.'s (2025) vision paper on rethinking government for the Agentic AI era.

## Key Points at a Glance

| Concept | Application to Government AI |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| Agentic State (Milgram) | Officials deferring to algorithmic recommendations |
| Agentic State (Ilves et al.) | Government transformation via autonomous AI systems |
| Automation Bias | Over-reliance on AI recommendations despite errors |
| Algorithmic Authority | Perceived legitimacy of automated decision-making |
| Moral Strain | Officials knowing AI is wrong but following it anyway |
| Responsibility Gap | "The algorithm decided" as modern "just following orders" |
